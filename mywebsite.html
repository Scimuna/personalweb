About me

Following my undergraduate studies, I taught Biology in a secondary school and later worked as a research assistant in Biological Science Department at Landmark University. This position allowed me to guide and support the department’s undergraduate students with research that involved testing antimicrobial potencies of extracts from different plants. In this position, I assisted students with the microbial culture and the extraction of bioactive substances from plants. Also, in an attempt to utilise farm waste at the university, I used lignocellulosic farm waste for biogas production, using a biodigester made from commonplace materials. Nevertheless, my first attempt failed to produce ample biogas from a large quantity of waste. I surmised that the recalcitrance of the feedstock might have caused this problem. In an effort to reconcile this, I collaborated with other scientists for research. We used fluted pumpkin fruit rind and poultry manure for biogas production.
